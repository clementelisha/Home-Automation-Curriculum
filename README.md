# Home-Automation-Curriculum

# Automation & Smart Systems

**Course Overview:** Learn how to make machines and homes work by themselves. This course covers everything from smart light switches to factory robots.

---

## Unit 1: Introduction to Automation
*Understanding how machines "think" and act without human help.*

* **1.1 What is Automation?**
    * The difference between manual control and automatic systems.
    * Benefits: Saving energy, improving safety, and making life easier.
* **1.2 The Brain of the System**
    * **Hubs and Controllers:** How a central brain (like Arduino or a Smart Hub) talks to all devices.
    * **Ecosystems:** A look at Google Home, Alexa, and Industrial PLC systems.

## Unit 2: Sensors (The Inputs)
*Giving your automation system "eyes" and "ears" to detect changes.*

* **2.1 Environmental Sensors**
    * **Temperature & Humidity:** Automatically turning on fans or heaters.
    * **Light Sensors (LDR):** Making streetlights turn on when it gets dark.
* **2.2 Safety & Security Sensors**
    * **Motion (PIR):** Detecting movement for security or auto-lighting.
    * **Gas & Smoke:** Detecting leaks to trigger alarms instantly.

![Arduino Sensors]([attachment_0](attachment))

---

## Unit 3: Switches and Actuators (The Outputs)
*How the system actually moves things or turns them on.*

* **3.1 High Voltage Control**
    * **Relays:** How a tiny 5V Arduino can safely switch a 220V wall outlet or light.
* **3.2 Mechanical Action**
    * **Solenoids:** Electronic locks that click open and shut.
    * **Smart Valves:** Turning water on or off for automatic garden watering.

![Relay Module]([attachment_2](attachment))

---

## Unit 4: Connectivity & IoT
*Connecting your system to the internet and your phone.*

* **4.1 Wireless Talk**
    * **Bluetooth:** Short-range control (for a single room).
    * **Wi-Fi (ESP8266/NodeMCU):** Connecting your project to your home router.
* **4.2 Mobile Apps & Dashboards**
    * **Blynk/IFTTT:** Creating buttons on your phone to control your house from anywhere in the world.

---

## Unit 5: Industrial & Advanced Automation
*Moving from the home to the factory.*

* **5.1 Professional Tools**
    * **PLCs (Programmable Logic Controllers):** The heavy-duty "Arduinos" used in real factories.
    * **HMI (Human Machine Interface):** The touchscreens workers use to control big machines.
* **5.2 Robotics in Industry**
    * **Conveyor Systems:** Sorting items automatically by color or size.
    * **Robotic Arms:** Precise movement for assembly lines.
